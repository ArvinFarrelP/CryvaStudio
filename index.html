Methodology

How we approach cryptanalysis

Our research process is systematic — combining statistical rigor with interactive experimentation to decode classical ciphers reliably.

01

Statistical Cryptanalysis

We apply frequency analysis grounded in linguistic statistics — modeling letter, bigram, and trigram distributions to identify substitution patterns in ciphertext.

02

Pattern Recognition

Beyond simple frequency counts, we detect structural patterns — repeated sequences, word boundaries, and positional bias — to narrow the cipher key space rapidly.

03

Classical Cipher Research

Deep study of Caesar, Atbash, Vigenère, and affine ciphers — understanding their mathematical structure to build targeted automated solvers.

04

Experimental Tool Development

Each research hypothesis is validated by building a working tool. If the tool decrypts real ciphertext reliably, the method is sound. Code is the proof.

Showcase

What CipherVision can do

Three core capabilities that make CipherVision a complete classical cipher workbench.

📊

Frequency Analysis

Feed in any ciphertext and get an instant letter frequency breakdown, ranked against standard English distribution — spotting E, T, A substitutes in seconds.

# frequency output
E: ██████████ 12.8%
T: ████████ 9.1%
A: ███████ 8.2%
→ Best match: shift = 3
⚡

Caesar Brute Force

Automatically test all 26 possible Caesar shifts and score each result against a language model. The correct plaintext surfaces to the top with confidence ratings.

# brute force results
[shift=3] HELLO WORLD ⭐ 98%
[shift=7] DAHHK SKNHZ 12%
[shift=19] MJQQT BTWQI 8%
✓ Decrypted successfully
🗺️

Manual Cipher Mapping

For complex substitution ciphers, manually remap each letter with live preview. Fine-tune your hypothesis character by character and see the plaintext emerge in real time.

# mapping table
K → H   H → E   R → O
U → R   O → L   G → D
